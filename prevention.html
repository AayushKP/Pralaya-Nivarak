Earthquake

An earthquake is a sudden and violent shaking of the Earth's crust caused by the release of energy stored in the Earth's tectonic plates.
02

Floods

A flood is an overflow of water onto normally dry land, often due to heavy rainfall, storm surges, or the melting of snow or ice, leading to inundation of areas and potential damage.
03

Cyclones

Hurricanes, typhoons, and cyclones are powerful tropical storms characterized by strong winds, heavy rainfall, and storm surges, forming over warm ocean waters and potentially causing widespread destruction.
04

Nuclear Accidents

These occur due to the release of harmful levels of radioactive materials from nuclear power plants, research facilities, or nuclear weapons testing, resulting in environmental contamination and health risks.
05

Industrial Accidents

These encompass events like chemical spills, explosions, or structural failures in industrial facilities, such as factories or chemical plants, leading to pollution, fires, and potential harm to human health and the environment.
